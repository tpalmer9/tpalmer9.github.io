Lichen-dripping

By weathered crossroads and tattered wood sign, Lena Lake leaves its dayhikers behind. The trail narrows, ceding to creeping green, blown spores since grown, and limbs, delicately adorned — this algaefungus symbiosis, draping almost to floor. Usnea, I recall the genus. That’s all. The name will never be of use. Today, its life is a referent. Today, to me, its life, a symbol I once read. But these gradients: sweeping grey/like-sage/like-mint/like-pistachio These textures: filigree filaments—Some spun silk, some knotted noise These rhythms: Threads spread across baroque, barked architecture. The name will never be of use. Stopping/Standing/Staring, still as I can, still puffing/pumping/cramping/crunching, still breathing/beating/bleating/blushing/rushing. Still much, much too synth for the humid-hungry, the sulfur-sensitive, for the all-absorbing — too brass for The Valley of Silent Men and it’s bearded old growth — too staccato for the creek-carved-hollow rock grotto running down its ravine — too mezzopiano to hear all the drips of these sick and dying trees.
